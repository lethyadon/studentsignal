# Why Would a School Keep Paying for StudentSignal Instead of Just Using Arbor, CPOMS and ClassCharts?

---

## The honest answer, stated directly

Schools keep Arbor, CPOMS and ClassCharts because the government and their data 
workflows require them to. They will keep paying for StudentSignal because it 
is the only place that tells them what to do with all that data.

---

## What Arbor, CPOMS and ClassCharts actually do

| System | What it does |
|---|---|
| Arbor | Records attendance and MIS data. Produces statutory reports. |
| CPOMS | Records safeguarding concerns. Produces an audit trail. |
| ClassCharts | Records behaviour points. Produces a chart. |

None of them answer: **Who needs attention today, why, and what should I do first?**

A DSL opening CPOMS at 8am sees a list of logged incidents sorted by date. She 
knows which concern she entered. She does not know which is escalating, which is 
resolved, which has a cross-source pattern she hasn't seen, or which of her seven 
open CPOMS cases needs her attention today and why.

A Head of Year opening Arbor sees attendance percentages. She doesn't know whether 
Jack Thomas's attendance is isolated, part of a peer cluster, connected to a home 
circumstance flagged in CPOMS two weeks ago, or whether his behaviour in Maths last 
week correlates with something his form tutor noticed.

A Headteacher opening ClassCharts sees behaviour points by form group. She doesn't 
know whether the spike in Year 9 is driven by two pupils, a particular teacher, 
a corridor, or a timetable change.

## The specific advantages over the existing tools

### 1. Cross-source correlation that staff cannot do manually

A teacher logging a CPOMS concern does not know whether it matches a Bromcom 
behaviour pattern from two weeks ago, an Arbor attendance drop from last month, 
or a pastoral note from the tutor. StudentSignal connects these automatically 
and surfaces the cross-source pattern as a single narrative.

No pastoral professional has time to do this manually across 200 pupils.

### 2. The WHY, not just the WHAT

Arbor tells you attendance is 82.4%. StudentSignal tells you it was 94.1% three 
weeks ago, fell after the safeguarding disclosure, and correlates with four late 
marks that appeared in the same fortnight — and that this pattern typically reaches 
the persistent absence threshold within two more weeks if unaddressed.

### 3. Predicted escalation

No existing system tells staff what will likely happen if they do nothing. 
StudentSignal does. This changes the conversation from "is this urgent?" to 
"we already know what happens if we wait — so we're not waiting."

### 4. Personal workload, not a global feed

Arbor shows all attendance. CPOMS shows all concerns. ClassCharts shows all 
behaviour. A Head of Year with 200 students in her year group sees everything — 
which means she must filter it herself to find what's hers.

StudentSignal shows each user only the pupils where they have a current, 
authorised, actionable responsibility. Nothing else. A teacher with no assigned 
actions sees no pupils. A HOY who has completed her three actions for the day 
sees no red pupils — even if the DSL still has seven open.

### 5. Pre-populated actions — zero re-entry

When a DSL accepts a safeguarding signal, the modal opens with:
- Pupil name already filled
- Action type already selected (Safeguarding review)
- Her own name already in the assignee field
- Priority already set (urgent)
- Due date already set (tomorrow)
- Evidence already attached (the three source records that triggered the signal)
- Success criteria already written
- Routing rationale already visible: "Auto-assigned to Ms Dalton because this 
  signal contains an open safeguarding concern and she is an authorised DSL."

She confirms in one click. She does not re-enter anything.

### 6. Historical intelligence that builds over time

As interventions are completed, StudentSignal records outcomes. Over time it can 
surface patterns like:
- This intervention programme outperforms the alternative in this year group
- This teacher's form group consistently improves after structured tutor time
- Three consecutive failed interventions for this pupil — escalate to external referral

No individual member of staff can hold this knowledge. StudentSignal can.

## What StudentSignal does not replace

StudentSignal does not replace CPOMS, Arbor or ClassCharts. Staff still log 
behaviour in ClassCharts. DSLs still record concerns in CPOMS. Attendance is 
still managed in Arbor.

StudentSignal reads all of them, connects them, and returns the intelligence 
that the tools themselves cannot produce.

The MIS systems become inputs. StudentSignal becomes the starting point.

## The one-sentence answer

**Schools keep paying for StudentSignal because it is the only system that tells 
every pastoral professional, every morning, exactly who needs attention, why, 
what to do about it, and what will happen if they don't — without them having 
to open any other system first.**

---

## Known limitations (honest)

- Historical learning (which interventions worked previously, which staff 
  consistently improve outcomes) requires 2–3 terms of intervention data to 
  become reliable. In the first term, predictions are based on published research 
  patterns, not school-specific history.
- The system is only as good as the data schools put into it. Poor-quality 
  ClassCharts recording produces weaker signal intelligence.
- Staff training flags (reward inflation, recording differentials) are advisory 
  only and require a human leader to decide whether and how to act.
- The "grey" (insufficient evidence) state will appear more often in the first 
  weeks of use, before all data sources are connected.
